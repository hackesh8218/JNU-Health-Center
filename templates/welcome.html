JNU Health Centre

Serving the JNU Community, New Delhi

The JNU Health Centre, established in 1973, serves as the primary medical care unit for students residing on the campus in New Delhi, focusing on comprehensive health services including preventive, curative, and promotive care. Located southwest of Ganga Hostel, the Centre is staffed by a dedicated team comprising a Chairperson, Chief Medical Officer (CMO), Medical Officers, nurses, and various specialists covering areas like Internal Medicine, Psychiatry, Cardiology, and Dentistry. The facility provides essential benefits such as free medicines to students, routine laboratory tests, and access to an OPD (with compulsory registration) operating in both morning and evening shifts. Additionally, the Centre ensures the safety of resident students with 24-hour ICU and Patient Transport Ambulance services, although specific services like advanced dental work and employment medical certifications are not covered.

Mission & Services

The mission of the JNU Health Centre is to provide basic, preventive, and curative healthcare services to all students in a convenient and accessible manner. The Centre aims to ensure the well-being of students by offering essential medical support within the campus.

Curative services for University employees are provided separately through the CGHS dispensary.

  • General medical consultation for students
  • Preventive care and routine health check-ups
  • Basic diagnostic tests
  • Primary treatment and first-aid services
  • Referral to CGHS or hospitals when needed

Location & Administration

Location:

Located about 300m from the North Gate in South West of Ganga Hostel.

Staffing Highlights:

  • Headed by the Chief Medical Officer.
  • Includes CMO, Medical Officer, part-time Doctors for General OPD.
  • Specialists, Homeopathic Physician, Nurses, Pharmacists, and Technical Staff.

OPD & Specialist Consultations

OPD registration is compulsory.

Available Specialists:

  • Internal Medicine, Psychiatry, ENT, Ophthalmology.
  • Dentistry, Skin, Cardiology.
  • Homeopathic Physician (services available).

Working Hours & Timings

General OPD (Mon-Sat):

Morning: 8:00 AM to 2:30 PM
Evening: 2:30 PM to 9:00 PM (Part-time doctors provide services)

Holidays:

Closed on Holi, Diwali, Sundays, and National Holidays.
Exception: Health Centre functions 8:00 AM to 2:30 PM on Gazetted holidays and Sundays.

Facilities & Fees

  • Medicines provided free of cost to students and retired employees (as per University rule).
  • Laboratory facility for Haematological, Biochemical and Microbiology tests (8 AM to 11 AM).
  • Free services ECG, Injection, and Dressing.
  • free Health servicefor all full-time and part-time students.

Ambulance & Services Not Covered

Ambulance Availability:

One ICU Ambulance and one Patient Transport Vehicle available 24 hours for resident students.

Services NOT Covered:

  • Dental services like denture, root canal treatment.
  • Medical examination/certifications for employment, insurance, legal, or non-academic purpose.
  • Laboratory tests/X-ray for purpose other than diagnosis and treatment.
  • No reimbursement permissible for students (as per UGC norms).
